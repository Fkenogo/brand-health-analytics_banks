# 1. USAGE FUNNEL MODEL

## The Complete Usage Cascade

```
AWARE (100%)
    ↓
    │ Trial Conversion
    ↓
EVER USED (50%)
    ↓
    │ Retention
    ↓
CURRENTLY USING (40%)
    ↓
    │ Preference Capture
    ↓
PREFERRED BANK / BUMO (15%)
```

## Funnel Logic

**Stage 1: AWARE**
- Baseline: Everyone who knows the bank exists
- Source: Section C, Q3 (Total Awareness)
- Size: 100% (of aware respondents)

**Stage 2: EVER USED**
- Definition: Have opened account or used services
- Source: Section D, Q1
- Conversion from Aware: "Trial Rate"

**Stage 3: CURRENTLY USING**
- Definition: Active account holders
- Source: Section D, Q2
- Conversion from Ever Used: "Retention Rate"

**Stage 4: PREFERRED BANK (BUMO)**
- Definition: Primary/main bank relationship
- Source: Section D, Q3
- Conversion from Current: "Preference Rate"

# 2. CORE USAGE METRICS

## 2.1 Ever Used (Trial Rate)

### Definition
Percentage of aware people who have ever tried the bank.

### Formula
```
Trial Rate = (Ever Used ÷ Aware) × 100
```

**Important:** Calculate as % of AWARE, not total sample.

### Data Source
**Your Questionnaire:**
- Section D, Q1: "Which banks have you EVER used?"
- Multiple selection allowed
- Denominator: Section C, Q3 (Aware respondents)

### Interpretation Levels
- **60%+:** Excellent conversion (most aware people try you)
- **40-59%:** Good conversion
- **25-39%:** Moderate conversion
- **10-24%:** Weak conversion (awareness not converting)
- **<10%:** Poor conversion (major barrier to trial)

### What Drives Trial
- Ease of account opening
- Branch accessibility
- Promotions and offers
- Recommendations from others
- Product relevance

---

## 2.2 Currently Using (Active Users)

### Definition
Percentage of aware people with active accounts.

### Formulas

**As % of Aware:**
```
Current Usage Rate = (Currently Using ÷ Aware) × 100
```

**As % of Total Market (Penetration):**
```
Market Penetration = (Currently Using ÷ Total Sample) × 100
```

### Data Source
**Your Questionnaire:**
- Section D, Q2: "Which banks are you CURRENTLY using?"
- Multiple selection allowed (multi-banking)

### Interpretation

**Current Usage Rate (of aware):**
- **50%+:** Excellent activation
- **30-49%:** Good activation
- **20-29%:** Moderate activation
- **10-19%:** Weak activation
- **<10%:** Poor activation

**Market Penetration (of total):**
- **30%+:** Market leader
- **20-29%:** Strong player
- **10-19%:** Moderate player
- **5-9%:** Niche player
- **<5%:** Small player

---

## 2.3 Preferred Bank (BUMO - Bank Used Most Often)

### Definition
The ONE bank respondents use most frequently for transactions.

### Formula
```
Preference Rate = (Preferred ÷ Currently Using) × 100

Also:
BUMO Penetration = (Preferred ÷ Aware) × 100
```

### Data Source
**Your Questionnaire:**
- Section D, Q3: "Which ONE bank do you use most often for your day-to-day banking needs?"
- Single selection only
- Auto-skipped if only using one bank

### Interpretation

**Preference Rate (of current users):**
- **60%+:** Strong primary relationships
- **40-59%:** Good primary conversion
- **25-39%:** Moderate (lots of secondary users)
- **15-24%:** Weak (mostly secondary accounts)
- **<15%:** Very weak (almost all secondary)

### Strategic Importance

**High Current Usage but Low Preference:**
- Many customers but mostly as secondary bank
- Lower engagement and revenue per customer
- Higher churn risk
- Lower wallet share

**High Preference Rate:**
- Deep customer relationships
- Higher transaction volumes
- More products per customer
- Better retention

---

## 2.4 Multi-Banking Rate

### Definition
Average number of banks customers use simultaneously.

### Formula
```
Avg Banks per User = Total active accounts ÷ Users with ≥1 account

Also calculate:
Multi-Banking % = (Users with 2+ banks ÷ All users) × 100
```

### Data Source
Count how many banks each respondent selected in Section D, Q2.

### Interpretation

**Average Banks:**
- 1.0-1.5: Low multi-banking (exclusive relationships)
- 1.6-2.5: Moderate multi-banking (typical)
- 2.6-3.5: High multi-banking
- 3.6+: Very high multi-banking

**Context:**
- Developed markets: 2-3 banks average
- Emerging markets: 1.5-2 banks average

### Implications

**Low Multi-Banking in Your Customer Base:**
- Good: High loyalty, primary relationships
- Bad: Limited cross-selling opportunities

**High Multi-Banking:**
- Good: Easy to acquire (customers open to new banks)
- Bad: Lower loyalty, competition for wallet share

# 3. CONVERSION & RETENTION

## 3.1 Trial Conversion Rate

### Definition
How effectively you convert awareness into trial.

### Formula
```
Trial Rate = (Ever Used ÷ Aware) × 100
```

### Benchmark Standards
- **Excellent:** >50% (half of aware people try you)
- **Good:** 30-50%
- **Average:** 20-30%
- **Poor:** <20%

### Improvement Levers

**If Trial Rate is Low (<25%):**
- Barrier analysis needed
- Check: Account opening process, documentation, fees
- Action: Simplify onboarding, reduce friction

**If Trial Rate is High (>50%) but Current is Low:**
- Retention problem, not acquisition problem
- Focus on service quality, not awareness

---

## 3.2 Retention Rate

### Definition
Percentage of triers who remain active customers.

### Formula
```
Retention Rate = (Currently Using ÷ Ever Used) × 100
```

### Data Source
- Numerator: Section D, Q2 (Currently Using)
- Denominator: Section D, Q1 (Ever Used)

### Benchmark Standards
- **Excellent:** >80%
- **Good:** 65-80%
- **Average:** 50-65%
- **Poor:** <50%

### Churn Calculation
```
Churn Rate = 100% - Retention Rate

Or:
Churned Users = Ever Used - Currently Using
Churn Rate = (Churned Users ÷ Ever Used) × 100
```

### Strategic Implications

**High Churn (>35%):**
- Customer experience issues
- Product/service not meeting needs
- Better competitive alternatives
- Fees too high relative to value

**Low Churn (<20%):**
- Good product-market fit
- Strong customer satisfaction
- Switching costs are effective
- Loyal customer base

---

## 3.3 Preference Capture Rate

### Definition
Ability to convert active users into primary bank relationships.

### Formula
```
Preference Rate = (Preferred ÷ Currently Using) × 100
```

### Benchmark Standards
- **Excellent:** >50% (majority are primary customers)
- **Good:** 35-50%
- **Average:** 25-35%
- **Poor:** <25% (mostly secondary accounts)

### Improvement Strategies

**To Increase Preference Rate:**
1. Product breadth (offer complete suite)
2. Relationship pricing (better rates for primary customers)
3. Digital excellence (easiest to use daily)
4. Switching incentives (consolidate to us)
5. Salary account partnerships

# 4. MULTI-BANKING ANALYSIS

## 4.1 Banking Portfolio Segmentation

**Segment customers by number of banks:**

```
Single-Bankers: Use only 1 bank (100% loyalty)
Dual-Bankers: Use exactly 2 banks (50% wallet share max)
Multi-Bankers: Use 3+ banks (<33% wallet share)
```

### Calculation
For each respondent, count selections in Section D, Q2:
```
IF count = 1: Single-Banker
IF count = 2: Dual-Banker
IF count ≥ 3: Multi-Banker
```

### Strategic Value

**Single-Bankers who chose YOU:**
- Highest value customers
- 100% wallet share
- Lowest acquisition cost (no competition)
- Protect these customers!

**Multi-Bankers:**
- Opportunity to increase share
- But also at risk of complete switching
- Need to become their primary bank

---

## 4.2 Your Position in Multi-Banking

**Among customers who use multiple banks:**

```
Primary Bank %: You are their BUMO
Secondary Bank %: You are NOT their BUMO
```

### Formula
```
Among your current users who also use other banks:

Primary Position = (Your BUMO ÷ Your multi-bankers) × 100
```

### Target
At least 40% of your multi-banking customers should consider you their primary bank.

---

## 4.3 Competitive Overlap Analysis

**Which other banks do YOUR customers also use?**

### Calculation
For each respondent currently using your bank:
- Check which OTHER banks they selected in Q2
- Count frequency of each competitor

### Strategic Use

**High overlap with Competitor X:**
- They're your direct competitive set
- Customers split wallets between you and them
- Study their strengths to compete better

**Low overlap:**
- Serving different needs/segments
- Less direct competition

# 5. CUSTOMER SEGMENTS

## 5.1 Usage-Based Segmentation

### Non-Triers
- **Definition:** Aware but never used
- **Size:** Aware - Ever Used
- **Opportunity:** Trial conversion campaigns

### Lapsed Users
- **Definition:** Used before, no longer active
- **Size:** Ever Used - Currently Using
- **Opportunity:** Win-back campaigns
- **Risk:** They left for a reason (likely dissatisfied)

### Secondary Users
- **Definition:** Currently using but NOT preferred
- **Size:** Currently Using - Preferred
- **Opportunity:** Primary bank conversion
- **Risk:** Lower engagement, higher churn risk

### Primary Users (BUMO)
- **Definition:** Preferred/main bank
- **Size:** Preferred count
- **Value:** Highest revenue, lowest churn
- **Strategy:** Retain and expand relationship

---

## 5.2 Segment Priorities

**Priority 1: PROTECT Primary Users**
- Highest lifetime value
- Lowest acquisition cost (already yours)
- Monitor satisfaction closely
- Expand product holdings

**Priority 2: CONVERT Secondary to Primary**
- Already have account (low barrier)
- Increase wallet share
- Make switching from competitor easy

**Priority 3: WIN BACK Lapsed Users**
- Address reasons for leaving
- Prove you've improved
- Special reactivation offers

**Priority 4: ACTIVATE Non-Triers**
- Remove barriers to account opening
- Targeted trial campaigns
- New customer incentives

# 6. DROP-OFF ANALYSIS

## 6.1 Stage-by-Stage Drop-offs

**Track losses at each funnel stage:**

### Drop-off 1: Aware → Ever Used
```
Lost Customers = Aware - Ever Used
Drop-off % = (Lost ÷ Aware) × 100
```

**Typical loss:** 40-60%
**If higher:** Trial conversion problem
**Root causes:** Barriers to account opening, lack of relevance

### Drop-off 2: Ever Used → Currently Using
```
Churned Customers = Ever Used - Currently Using
Churn % = (Churned ÷ Ever Used) × 100
```

**Typical loss:** 20-35%
**If higher:** Retention problem
**Root causes:** Poor experience, better alternatives

### Drop-off 3: Currently Using → Preferred
```
Secondary Users = Currently Using - Preferred
Secondary % = (Secondary ÷ Currently Using) × 100
```

**Typical loss:** 50-70%
**If higher:** Preference problem
**Root causes:** Incomplete product suite, not daily-use bank

---

## 6.2 Friction Point Identification

**Calculate friction score for each stage:**

```
Friction Score = Drop-off % × Stage Importance

Stage Weights (example):
Aware → Trial: 1.0
Trial → Active: 2.0 (retention more important)
Active → Preferred: 1.5
```

**Focus resources on highest friction stages.**

# 7. STRATEGIC INSIGHTS

## 7.1 Funnel Health Diagnosis

### Healthy Funnel Pattern
```
Aware: 70%
Ever Used: 35% (50% trial rate) ✓
Currently Using: 28% (80% retention) ✓
Preferred: 12% (43% preference) ✓

Steady conversion at each stage
High retention (low churn)
Reasonable primary bank capture
```

### Problem Pattern 1: "Awareness Doesn't Convert"
```
Aware: 75%
Ever Used: 15% (20% trial rate) ✗
Currently Using: 12% (80% retention) ✓
Preferred: 6% (50% preference) ✓

Problem: Low trial despite high awareness
Cause: Barriers to entry, lack of relevance
Action: Simplify onboarding, trial campaigns
```

### Problem Pattern 2: "Leaky Bucket"
```
Aware: 70%
Ever Used: 42% (60% trial rate) ✓
Currently Using: 17% (40% retention) ✗
Preferred: 9% (53% preference) ✓

Problem: Good acquisition, terrible retention
Cause: Poor customer experience
Action: Fix service quality, product issues
```

### Problem Pattern 3: "Secondary Bank Syndrome"
```
Aware: 70%
Ever Used: 35% (50% trial rate) ✓
Currently Using: 28% (80% retention) ✓
Preferred: 4% (14% preference) ✗

Problem: Many customers but almost all secondary
Cause: Incomplete product suite, not daily use
Action: Cross-sell, relationship pricing
```

## 7.2 Competitive Usage Insights

### Market Position Analysis

**Calculate for all banks:**
- Current Usage %
- Trial Rate
- Retention Rate
- Preference Rate

**Position Matrix:**

```
High Usage, High Retention = Market Leader
High Usage, Low Retention = Vulnerable Leader
Low Usage, High Retention = Growing Challenger
Low Usage, Low Retention = Struggling Brand
```

---

## 7.3 Growth Opportunity Sizing

**Calculate addressable market at each stage:**

### Opportunity 1: Convert Non-Triers
```
Size = Aware - Ever Used
Revenue Potential = Size × Avg Account Value × Trial Likelihood
```

### Opportunity 2: Reactivate Lapsed
```
Size = Ever Used - Currently Using
Revenue Potential = Size × Avg Account Value × Win-back Rate
```

### Opportunity 3: Primary Conversion
```
Size = Currently Using - Preferred
Revenue Potential = Size × (Primary Value - Secondary Value)
```

**Prioritize based on:**
- Size of opportunity
- Conversion probability
- Revenue impact
- Resource requirements

## Decision Framework

### When Trial Rate is Low (<30%)
**Focus:** Acquisition campaigns
**Tactics:** 
- Simplified onboarding
- Trial offers/promotions
- Address barriers to entry
- Increase relevance messaging

### When Retention is Low (<65%)
**Focus:** Customer experience
**Tactics:**
- Service quality improvement
- Product/feature enhancements
- Proactive churn prevention
- Win-back campaigns

### When Preference is Low (<30%)
**Focus:** Relationship deepening
**Tactics:**
- Cross-sell additional products
- Relationship pricing
- Make daily banking easier
- Increase switching costs from competitors

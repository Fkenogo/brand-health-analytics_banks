# 1. CORE AWARENESS METRICS

## 1.1 Total Awareness (Aided + Unaided)

### Definition
Percentage of your target market who recognize your brand name when asked, either spontaneously or when shown a list.

### Formula
```
Total Awareness = (Number who know the brand ÷ Total sample) × 100
```

### Data Source
**Your Questionnaire:**
- Section C, Q1: Top-of-Mind (unaided)
- Section C, Q2: Spontaneous Recall (unaided)
- Section C, Q3: Aided Awareness (checklist)

**Logic:**
```
IF respondent mentioned brand in Q1 OR Q2 OR selected in Q3:
    Aware = TRUE
```

### Interpretation Levels
- **90-100%:** Market leader, dominant presence
- **70-89%:** Strong established brand
- **50-69%:** Moderate awareness, growing brand
- **30-49%:** Emerging brand, needs investment
- **0-29%:** New entrant or struggling brand

### What Drives Total Awareness
- Marketing spend and SOV (Share of Voice)
- Time in market
- Branch/ATM visibility
- Word of mouth
- Media coverage and PR

---

## 1.2 Top-of-Mind Awareness (Unaided)

### Definition
The percentage of people who mention your brand FIRST when asked about banks in your country. This is the gold standard of brand awareness.

### Formula
```
Top-of-Mind Awareness = (First mentions ÷ Total sample) × 100
```

### Data Source
**Your Questionnaire:**
- Section C, Q1: "Which bank from your country comes to mind FIRST?"

**Important:** Each respondent can only have ONE first mention. This is mutually exclusive across banks.

### Interpretation Levels
- **30%+:** Clear market leader
- **20-29%:** Strong brand salience
- **10-19%:** Moderate mind-share
- **5-9%:** Weak but present
- **<5%:** Minimal brand salience

### Why Top-of-Mind Matters
Top-of-mind awareness is the strongest predictor of:
- Consideration set inclusion
- Choice at point of purchase
- Market share

**Research shows:** The brand that comes to mind first is 3-5x more likely to be chosen.

---

## 1.3 Spontaneous Recall (Unaided)

### Definition
Percentage who mention your brand without prompting, even if not first. This includes top-of-mind plus other unprompted mentions.

### Formula
```
Spontaneous Recall = (Unprompted mentions in Q1 or Q2 ÷ Total sample) × 100
```

### Data Source
**Your Questionnaire:**
- Section C, Q1: Top-of-Mind mention
- Section C, Q2: "Which OTHER banks come to mind?"

**Calculation Logic:**
```
Count UNIQUE respondents who mentioned brand in Q1 OR Q2
(Do NOT double-count if mentioned in both)
```

### Relationship to Top-of-Mind
```
Spontaneous Recall ≥ Top-of-Mind (always)

Because Spontaneous = Top-of-Mind + Other unprompted mentions
```

### Interpretation Levels
- **60%+:** Excellent unprompted awareness
- **40-59%:** Good spontaneous recognition
- **20-39%:** Moderate unprompted recall
- **10-19%:** Weak but measurable
- **<10%:** Minimal spontaneous awareness

---

## 1.4 Aided Awareness

### Definition
Recognition when shown a list of banks. This measures passive familiarity rather than active recall.

### Formula
```
Aided Awareness = (Selected from list ÷ Total sample) × 100
```

### Data Source
**Your Questionnaire:**
- Section C, Q3: "Tick all banks that you are aware of:"
  - Rwanda: 13 banks listed
  - Uganda: 28 banks listed
  - Burundi: 14 banks listed

### The Awareness Gap
```
Aided Awareness - Spontaneous Recall = Awareness Gap

Large gap (>30pp): Passive awareness only - people know name but don't think of you
Small gap (<15pp): Strong active awareness - top-of-mind for most who know you
```

### Interpretation
**Aided awareness alone is NOT enough.** It shows:
- Past marketing exposure
- Brand name recognition
- Passive familiarity

But it does NOT predict:
- Consideration
- Purchase intent
- Market share

---

## 1.5 Awareness Quality

### Definition
The ratio of top-of-mind awareness to total awareness. Measures depth of awareness penetration.

### Formula
```
Awareness Quality = (Top-of-Mind ÷ Total Awareness) × 100
```

### Data Source
**Derived from:** Q1 ÷ Q3 results

### Quality Tiers
- **40%+:** Excellent quality - strong brand salience
- **25-39%:** Good quality - healthy mind-share
- **15-24%:** Moderate quality - room for improvement
- **10-14%:** Weak quality - passive awareness only
- **<10%:** Poor quality - brand awareness is shallow

### Strategic Implications

**High Awareness, High Quality (>30%):**
- "Top-of-Mind Leader"
- Strategy: Maintain, defend market position
- Example: 80% aware, 30% top-of-mind = 37.5% quality

**High Awareness, Low Quality (<20%):**
- "Recognized but Forgotten"
- Strategy: Improve salience through distinctive campaigns
- Example: 80% aware, 10% top-of-mind = 12.5% quality
- Problem: People know you but don't think of you

**Low Awareness, High Quality (>30%):**
- "Hidden Gem"
- Strategy: Scale awareness campaigns
- Example: 30% aware, 12% top-of-mind = 40% quality
- Opportunity: Those who know you love you - grow reach

**Low Awareness, Low Quality (<20%):**
- "Struggle Brand"
- Strategy: Fundamental brand building needed
- Example: 30% aware, 3% top-of-mind = 10% quality

# 2. AWARENESS FUNNEL MODEL

## The Awareness Cascade

```
TOTAL SAMPLE (1,000 people)
        ↓
        │ Awareness Penetration
        ↓
TOTAL AWARENESS (720 people, 72%)
        ↓
        │ Depth of Awareness
        ↓
SPONTANEOUS RECALL (430 people, 43%)
        ↓
        │ Mind-Share Capture
        ↓
TOP-OF-MIND (150 people, 15%)
```

## Funnel Conversion Rates

### 1. Awareness Penetration Rate
```
Total Awareness ÷ Total Sample × 100
= 720 ÷ 1,000 = 72%
```
**What it means:** Market reach

### 2. Spontaneous Conversion Rate
```
Spontaneous Recall ÷ Total Awareness × 100
= 430 ÷ 720 = 59.7%
```
**What it means:** How many aware people actively recall you
**Benchmark:** Should be 50-70%

### 3. Top-of-Mind Conversion Rate
```
Top-of-Mind ÷ Spontaneous Recall × 100
= 150 ÷ 430 = 34.9%
```
**What it means:** Your share of mind among those who recall
**Benchmark:** Should be 25-45%

## Funnel Health Indicators

**Healthy Funnel:**
- Gradual decline at each stage
- No dramatic drop-offs
- High conversion rates (>50% at each stage)

**Problem Funnel:**
- Large drops between stages
- Very wide bottom (high aided, low spontaneous)
- Low top-of-mind conversion

# 3. COMPETITIVE AWARENESS BENCHMARKING

## 3.1 Market Awareness Rankings

### How to Rank
For each bank, calculate:
1. Total Awareness %
2. Top-of-Mind %
3. Awareness Quality %

Then rank banks from highest to lowest.

### Sample Ranking Table

| Rank | Bank | Total Aware | Top-Mind | Quality |
|------|------|-------------|----------|---------|
| 1 | BPR | 75% | 18% | 24.0% |
| 2 | BK | 72% | 15% | 20.8% |
| 3 | I&M | 68% | 12% | 17.6% |
| 4 | EcoBank | 58% | 8% | 13.8% |
| 5 | Access | 45% | 5% | 11.1% |

### Ranking Insights

**#1 in Total Awareness but not Top-of-Mind:**
- You're well-known but not preferred
- Need to improve brand salience and differentiation

**#1 in Top-of-Mind but lower Total Awareness:**
- Strong brand loyalty among those who know you
- Opportunity to scale awareness

**Rank improving over time:**
- Marketing campaigns working
- Growing brand momentum

**Rank declining over time:**
- Competitors gaining ground
- Need intervention

---

## 3.2 Share of Voice (SOV)

### Definition
Your proportion of total top-of-mind mentions in the market.

### Formula
```
Share of Voice = (Your top-of-mind mentions ÷ All top-of-mind mentions) × 100
```

### Calculation Example
```
Total sample: 1,000
Total top-of-mind mentions: 550 (excluding "Don't Know")

Your bank: 150 mentions

SOV = 150 ÷ 550 = 27.3%
```

### SOV vs Market Share Relationship

**Empirical Rule:**
```
IF SOV > Market Share: Growing brand (gaining share)
IF SOV = Market Share: Steady state (maintaining share)
IF SOV < Market Share: Declining brand (losing share)
```

**Example:**
```
Your SOV: 27%
Your Market Share: 22% (currently using ÷ total sample)

27% > 22% → You're gaining market momentum
```

---

## 3.3 Awareness Share Index

### Definition
Your share of total awareness in the market.

### Formula
```
Awareness Share = (Your total aware ÷ Sum of all banks' awareness) × 100
```

**Note:** Total can exceed 100% because people can be aware of multiple banks.

### Strategic Application

**High Awareness Share + Low Market Share:**
- Good awareness but poor conversion
- Focus on usage activation

**Low Awareness Share + High Market Share:**
- Loyal customer base but limited reach
- Focus on awareness expansion

# 4. AWARENESS QUALITY ASSESSMENT

## 4.1 The Awareness Pyramid

```
               Top-of-Mind (15%)
              ╱                 ╲
             ╱   Most Valuable   ╲
            ╱                     ╲
           ├───────────────────────┤
          ╱  Spontaneous (43%)     ╲
         ╱                          ╲
        ╱    Moderate Value          ╲
       ╱                              ╲
      ├────────────────────────────────┤
     ╱    Total Awareness (72%)        ╲
    ╱                                   ╲
   ╱         Lowest Value                ╲
  ╱                                       ╲
 └─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Value decreases from top to bottom:**
- Top-of-Mind = Highest conversion potential
- Spontaneous = Moderate conversion potential
- Aided only = Lowest conversion potential

## 4.2 Awareness Depth Score

### Formula
```
Depth Score = (Top-of-Mind × 3) + (Spontaneous × 2) + (Aided only × 1)
              ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                                Total Sample × 3
```

### Example Calculation
```
Sample: 1,000
Top-of-Mind: 150 (× 3 = 450 points)
Spontaneous: 280 (× 2 = 560 points)
Aided only: 290 (× 1 = 290 points)

Depth Score = (450 + 560 + 290) ÷ (1,000 × 3)
            = 1,300 ÷ 3,000
            = 0.433 or 43.3/100
```

**Interpretation:**
- 60-100: Excellent depth
- 40-59: Good depth
- 20-39: Moderate depth
- 0-19: Weak depth

# 5. DEMOGRAPHIC ANALYSIS

## 5.1 Awareness by Age

### Calculation
For each age group:
```
Age Group Awareness = (Aware in age group ÷ Total in age group) × 100
```

### Sample Results
```
Age 18-24: 450 ÷ 800 = 56.3% awareness
Age 25-34: 620 ÷ 900 = 68.9% awareness
Age 35-44: 580 ÷ 700 = 82.9% awareness
Age 45-54: 420 ÷ 450 = 93.3% awareness
Age 55+: 230 ÷ 150 = 66.7% awareness
```

### Insights from Age Analysis

**Awareness increases with age:**
- Natural pattern (older = more exposure)
- Young adults are opportunity segment

**Awareness peaks mid-life then declines:**
- Core banking age (35-54) has highest awareness
- Focus retention efforts here

**Youth awareness very low (<40%):**
- Missing future customers
- Need youth-focused campaigns

---

## 5.2 Awareness by Gender

### Calculation
```
Male Awareness = (Aware males ÷ Total males) × 100
Female Awareness = (Aware females ÷ Total females) × 100
```

### Gender Gap Analysis
```
Gender Gap = Male Awareness - Female Awareness
```

### Interpretation

**Large gap (>15pp):**
- Indicates gender bias in marketing or product
- Example: 75% male vs 55% female = 20pp gap
- Action: Review messaging, channels, product features

**Small gap (<5pp):**
- Gender-neutral brand
- Balanced marketing approach

---

## 5.3 Awareness by Other Demos

**Employment Status:**

```
Employed awareness vs Unemployed awareness
Self-employed awareness (often higher - business needs)
```

**Education Level:**
```
Higher education typically = higher awareness
Indicates information access and media consumption
```

# 6. TREND ANALYSIS

## 6.1 Month-over-Month (MoM) Change

### Formula
```
MoM Change (pp) = Current Period % - Previous Period %
MoM Change (%) = (Change pp ÷ Previous Period %) × 100
```

### Example
```
May: 68% awareness
June: 72% awareness

Change (pp) = 72 - 68 = +4 percentage points
Change (%) = (4 ÷ 68) × 100 = +5.9%
```

### Interpretation Thresholds

**Strong Growth:** >+3% per month
**Moderate Growth:** +1% to +3%
**Stable:** ±1%
**Declining:** -1% to -3%
**Crisis:** <-3% per month

---

## 6.2 6-Month Trend Analysis

### Calculation
Track awareness for 6 consecutive months:
```
Jan: 68%
Feb: 70%
Mar: 69%
Apr: 71%
May: 73%
Jun: 75%
```

### Trend Metrics

**1. Total Change:**
```
Jun - Jan = 75 - 68 = +7 percentage points
```

**2. Average Monthly Change:**
```
(+2, -1, +2, +2, +2) = +7 over 5 months
Average = +7 ÷ 5 = +1.4 pp/month
```

**3. Trend Direction:**
```
Linear regression slope
IF slope > 0: Upward trend
IF slope < 0: Downward trend
```

**4. Volatility:**
```
Standard Deviation of monthly values
Low volatility (<3): Stable, predictable
High volatility (>5): Erratic, investigate causes
```

---

## 6.3 Seasonality Detection

### Method
Compare same month across multiple years:
```
Jun 2024: 68%
Jun 2025: 72%
Jun 2026: 75%

Average June awareness: 71.7%
```

### Seasonal Patterns in Banking

**Typical patterns:**
- **Q1 (Jan-Mar):** Post-holiday dip
- **Q2 (Apr-Jun):** Recovery, tax season boost
- **Q3 (Jul-Sep):** Summer stability
- **Q4 (Oct-Dec):** Year-end peak, then Dec decline

**Adjust strategies accordingly:**
- Increase marketing in low seasons
- Capitalize on high seasons

# 7. INSIGHTS & RECOMMENDATIONS

## 7.1 Automated Insight Generation

### High Awareness, Low Quality
```
IF Total Awareness > 60% AND Awareness Quality < 20%:
    Insight: "BK is well-known but not top-of-mind. 
              People recognize the name but don't think of you first."
    
    Recommendation: "Focus on distinctive campaigns that create 
                     mental availability. Consider memorable taglines,
                     unique positioning, or disruptive creative."
```

### Low Awareness, High Quality
```
IF Total Awareness < 40% AND Awareness Quality > 30%:
    Insight: "BK has strong brand salience among those who know you,
              but limited market reach."
    
    Recommendation: "Scale awareness campaigns. You've proven you can
                     create memorable impressions - now expand reach
                     through mass media, partnerships, or branch expansion."
```

### Declining Awareness
```
IF 6-month trend shows >5% decline:
    Insight: "BK awareness is declining. Competitors may be gaining ground."
    
    Recommendation: "Urgent review of marketing spend and SOV.
                     Analyze competitor activities. Consider campaign refresh."
```

### Youth Gap
```
IF Age 18-34 awareness < Overall awareness by >15pp:
    Insight: "BK has weak penetration among young adults.
              This threatens future market position."
    
    Recommendation: "Launch youth-targeted campaigns on digital/social channels.
                     Review product offerings for young professional needs.
                     Partner with universities/youth organizations."
```

---

## 7.2 Strategic Decision Framework

### When Awareness is Low (<50%)
**Priority:** Build reach
**Tactics:**
- Mass media campaigns (TV, radio, billboards)
- Branch visibility expansion
- Sponsorships and partnerships
- PR and media coverage

### When Awareness is High (>70%) but Quality is Low (<20%)
**Priority:** Improve salience
**Tactics:**
- Distinctive creative campaigns
- Memorable taglines/jingles
- Unique brand positioning
- Emotional storytelling

### When Both Awareness and Quality are High
**Priority:** Maintain and defend
**Tactics:**
- Consistent brand presence
- Defend against competitor attacks
- Innovation to stay relevant
- Customer experience excellence

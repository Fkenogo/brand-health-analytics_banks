# 1. UNDERSTANDING BRAND MOMENTUM

## 1.1 What is Brand Momentum?

Brand Momentum measures whether your brand is **gaining or losing strength** over time. It combines:
- **Direction:** Are you moving up or down?
- **Velocity:** How fast are you moving?
- **Sustainability:** Can you maintain this trajectory?

**Key Principle:** A brand with strong momentum is growing across the entire customer journey, from initial awareness through to loyal advocacy.

---

## 1.2 Why Momentum Matters More Than Static Metrics

**Traditional Approach:**
- "We have 70% awareness" ← Static snapshot
- Tells you where you are, not where you're going

**Momentum Approach:**
- "We have 70% awareness, up from 65% (+5pp), accelerating" ← Dynamic trajectory
- Predicts future performance

**Research Shows:**
- Brands with positive momentum grow market share 2-3x faster
- Momentum is a leading indicator of financial performance
- Investors value momentum as much as current metrics

---

## 1.3 The Momentum Framework

```
AWARENESS GROWTH
    ↓
CONSIDERATION EXPANSION
    ↓
TRIAL CONVERSION
    ↓
CUSTOMER RETENTION
    ↓
PRIMARY ADOPTION
    ↓
= BRAND MOMENTUM
```

**Logic:** Strong brands show improvement at ALL stages, not just one.

# 2. THE FIVE COMPONENTS

## 2.1 Component Overview

**1. Awareness Growth (15% weight)**
- **What:** Change in brand awareness over time
- **Why:** Top of funnel growth drives future conversions
- **Measurement:** Period-over-period awareness change

**2. Consideration Rate (25% weight)**
- **What:** % of non-users with intent to use
- **Why:** Size of acquisition pipeline
- **Measurement:** High intent (7-10) among aware non-users

**3. Conversion Rate (25% weight)**
- **What:** Ability to convert awareness into trial
- **Why:** Marketing effectiveness
- **Measurement:** Ever Used ÷ Aware

**4. Retention Rate (20% weight)**
- **What:** Ability to keep customers active
- **Why:** Customer satisfaction and value delivery
- **Measurement:** Currently Using ÷ Ever Used

**5. Adoption Rate (15% weight)**
- **What:** Conversion to primary bank status
- **Why:** Depth of relationships and wallet share
- **Measurement:** Preferred ÷ Currently Using

---

## 2.2 Why These Five?

**Strategic Coverage:**
- **Awareness Growth:** Future pipeline
- **Consideration:** Near-term acquisition potential
- **Conversion:** Marketing & product effectiveness
- **Retention:** Customer experience quality
- **Adoption:** Relationship depth

**Together they measure:**
- ✓ Top-of-funnel strength
- ✓ Middle-of-funnel effectiveness
- ✓ Bottom-of-funnel loyalty
- ✓ Growth trajectory
- ✓ Competitive positioning

# 3. MOMENTUM SCORE CALCULATION

## 3.1 The Formula

```
Momentum Score = 
    (Awareness Growth Score × 15%) +
    (Consideration Rate × 25%) +
    (Conversion Rate × 25%) +
    (Retention Rate × 20%) +
    (Adoption Rate × 15%)

Score Range: 0-100
```

---

## 3.2 Component Score Normalization

### Component 1: Awareness Growth Score

**Raw Input:** Period-over-period percentage point change in awareness

**Normalization:**
```
IF growth >= +10pp: Score = 100
IF growth <= -10pp: Score = 0
ELSE: Score = 50 + (growth × 5)
```

**Example:**
```
Previous: 68% awareness
Current: 72% awareness
Growth: +4pp

Score = 50 + (4 × 5) = 70/100
```

**Rationale:** ±10pp change in a single period is exceptional; most brands see ±2-5pp changes.

---

### Components 2-5: Already 0-100 Scale

**These components are already percentages (0-100):**
- Consideration Rate: % with high intent
- Conversion Rate: Trial rate %
- Retention Rate: Retention %
- Adoption Rate: Preference %

**No normalization needed** - use raw percentages as scores.

---

## 3.3 Weighting Rationale

**Why these specific weights?**

**25% each: Consideration & Conversion**
- Highest weights (50% combined)
- Drive near-term growth
- Most actionable in short term

**20%: Retention**
- Critical for sustainability
- Prevents "leaky bucket"
- High impact on lifetime value

**15% each: Awareness Growth & Adoption**
- Lower weights (30% combined)
- Awareness: Longer-term impact
- Adoption: Lower priority than retention

---

## 3.4 Worked Example

**Component Values:**
```
1. Awareness Growth: Previous 68%, Current 72% (+4pp)
   Normalized: 50 + (4 × 5) = 70

2. Consideration Rate: 180 high-intent ÷ 432 non-users = 41.7%
   Score: 42

3. Conversion Rate: 360 ever used ÷ 720 aware = 50%
   Score: 50

4. Retention Rate: 288 current ÷ 360 ever used = 80%
   Score: 80

5. Adoption Rate: 108 preferred ÷ 288 current = 37.5%
   Score: 38
```

**Weighted Calculation:**
```
(70 × 0.15) = 10.5
(42 × 0.25) = 10.5
(50 × 0.25) = 12.5
(80 × 0.20) = 16.0
(38 × 0.15) = 5.7
─────────────────
Total: 55.2

Momentum Score: 55/100
```

# 4. COMPONENT DEEP DIVES

## 4.1 Awareness Growth Component

### Data Required
- Current period total awareness %
- Previous period total awareness %
- Data source: Section C, Q3 over time

### Calculation Steps
```
Step 1: Current awareness = 720 ÷ 1,000 = 72%
Step 2: Previous awareness = 680 ÷ 1,000 = 68%
Step 3: Growth = 72 - 68 = +4 percentage points
Step 4: Normalize = 50 + (4 × 5) = 70/100
```

### What Drives This?
- Marketing spend and SOV
- Campaign effectiveness
- Brand visibility (branches, ATMs, sponsorships)
- PR and media coverage
- Word of mouth

### Strategic Levers
**To Improve:**
- Increase media presence
- Launch high-visibility campaigns
- Expand physical presence
- Strategic partnerships
- Event sponsorships

---

## 4.2 Consideration Rate Component

### Data Required
- Non-users aware of bank
- Non-users with future intent 7-10
- Data source: Section D, Q1 (Future Intent)

### Calculation Steps
```
Step 1: Aware non-users = 720 aware - 288 using = 432
Step 2: High intent non-users = 180 (future intent 7-10)
Step 3: Consideration rate = 180 ÷ 432 = 41.7%
Step 4: Score = 42/100 (already normalized)
```

### What Drives This?
- Brand relevance
- Product attractiveness
- Competitive positioning
- Recent campaigns
- Recommendations received

### Strategic Levers
**To Improve:**
- Trial offers and promotions
- Testimonials and social proof
- Product innovation
- Remove barriers to trial
- Targeted messaging to high-intent segments

---

## 4.3 Conversion Rate Component

### Data Required
- Ever Used count
- Total Aware count
- Data source: Section D, Q1 (Ever Used), Section C, Q3 (Aware)

### Calculation Steps
```
Step 1: Ever used = 360
Step 2: Aware = 720
Step 3: Conversion rate = 360 ÷ 720 = 50%
Step 4: Score = 50/100 (already normalized)
```

### What Drives This?
- Account opening process ease
- Initial offer attractiveness
- Branch accessibility
- Digital onboarding experience
- Trust and credibility

### Strategic Levers
**To Improve:**
- Simplify account opening
- Improve digital onboarding
- Reduce documentation requirements
- Compelling trial offers
- Risk-free trial periods

---

## 4.4 Retention Rate Component

### Data Required
- Currently Using count
- Ever Used count
- Data source: Section D, Q2 (Current), Q1 (Ever Used)

### Calculation Steps
```
Step 1: Currently using = 288
Step 2: Ever used = 360
Step 3: Retention rate = 288 ÷ 360 = 80%
Step 4: Score = 80/100 (already normalized)
```

### What Drives This?
- Customer service quality
- Product performance
- Fee competitiveness
- Digital experience
- Problem resolution

### Strategic Levers
**To Improve:**
- Customer experience enhancement
- Proactive churn prevention
- Loyalty programs
- Regular engagement
- Service quality improvements

---

## 4.5 Adoption Rate Component

### Data Required
- Preferred Bank count
- Currently Using count
- Data source: Section D, Q3 (Preferred), Q2 (Current)

### Calculation Steps
```
Step 1: Preferred = 108
Step 2: Currently using = 288
Step 3: Adoption rate = 108 ÷ 288 = 37.5%
Step 4: Score = 38/100 (already normalized)
```

### What Drives This?
- Product breadth
- Cross-sell effectiveness
- Relationship pricing
- Digital superiority
- Switching costs from competitors

### Strategic Levers
**To Improve:**
- Cross-selling programs
- Bundle pricing
- Relationship rewards
- Primary bank incentives
- Make daily banking easier

# 5. MOMENTUM INTERPRETATION

## 5.1 Score Ranges

**80-100: Excellent Momentum**
- **Status:** Brand on strong growth trajectory
- **Characteristics:** Growing across all funnel stages
- **Strategic Posture:** Scale and defend
- **Example:** High awareness growth, strong consideration, excellent retention

**60-79: Good Momentum**
- **Status:** Healthy brand with room for improvement
- **Characteristics:** Strong in most areas, some weaknesses
- **Strategic Posture:** Optimize and grow
- **Example:** Good retention but weak adoption, or strong acquisition but moderate retention

**40-59: Moderate Momentum**
- **Status:** Mixed signals, needs attention
- **Characteristics:** Some strengths, significant weaknesses
- **Strategic Posture:** Fix and rebuild
- **Example:** Awareness declining but good retention, or growing awareness but poor conversion

**20-39: Weak Momentum**
- **Status:** Concerning trends
- **Characteristics:** Weaknesses across multiple areas
- **Strategic Posture:** Urgent intervention
- **Example:** Declining awareness, low consideration, poor retention

**0-19: Crisis Momentum**
- **Status:** Brand in serious trouble
- **Characteristics:** Failing across the board
- **Strategic Posture:** Emergency turnaround
- **Example:** Losing awareness, high churn, no new acquisition

# 6. MOMENTUM DRIVERS ANALYSIS

## 6.1 Contribution Analysis

**Calculate each component's contribution to total score:**

```
Component          Weight   Score   Contribution   % of Total
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Awareness Growth   15%      70      10.5           19%
Consideration      25%      42      10.5           19%
Conversion         25%      50      12.5           23%
Retention          20%      80      16.0           29% ← Biggest
Adoption           15%      38      5.7            10% ← Weakest
                                    ─────
                                    55.2 Total
```

**Strategic Insight:**
- **Biggest Driver:** Retention (29% of total) - your strength
- **Weakest Link:** Adoption (10% of total) - your opportunity

**Action Priority:** Focus on converting secondary users to primary bank status.

---

## 6.2 Sensitivity Analysis

**Which component improvements yield most momentum gain?**

**Scenario 1: Improve Adoption by 10 points**
```
New Adoption: 38 + 10 = 48
New Contribution: 48 × 0.15 = 7.2
Momentum Gain: 7.2 - 5.7 = +1.5 points

New Momentum: 55 + 1.5 = 56.5
```

**Scenario 2: Improve Consideration by 10 points**
```
New Consideration: 42 + 10 = 52
New Contribution: 52 × 0.25 = 13.0
Momentum Gain: 13.0 - 10.5 = +2.5 points

New Momentum: 55 + 2.5 = 57.5
```

**Conclusion:** Improving consideration (25% weight) has bigger impact than adoption (15% weight).

---

## 6.3 Investment Prioritization

**ROI Framework:**

```
Priority = (Potential Gain × Weight) ÷ Difficulty

Where:
Potential Gain = Gap to excellence (90 score)
Weight = Component weight %
Difficulty = 1-5 scale (1=easy, 5=hard)
```

**Example:**
```
Component          Gap    Weight   Difficulty   Priority Score
Awareness Growth   20     15%      3            1.0
Consideration      48     25%      2            6.0  ← Highest
Conversion         40     25%      3            3.3
Retention          10     20%      4            0.5
Adoption           52     15%      2            3.9

Rank by Priority:
1. Consideration (6.0) - Big gap, high weight, medium difficulty
2. Adoption (3.9) - Huge gap, low weight, easy to improve
3. Conversion (3.3) - Big gap, high weight, harder to improve
```

# 7. TREND & TRAJECTORY

## 7.1 Month-over-Month Momentum

**Track momentum score monthly:**

```
Month    Score   Change   Assessment
Jan      48      -        Baseline
Feb      50      +2       Slight improvement
Mar      51      +1       Steady
Apr      53      +2       Improving
May      54      +1       Steady
Jun      55      +1       Continuing improvement

6-Month Analysis:
Total Change: +7 points
Average Monthly: +1.4 points/month
Trend: Positive, gradual ✓
```

---

## 7.2 Momentum Velocity

**Measure rate of change:**

```
Velocity = (Recent 3-month avg) - (Earlier 3-month avg)

Recent (Apr-Jun): (53+54+55) ÷ 3 = 54.0
Earlier (Jan-Mar): (48+50+51) ÷ 3 = 49.7

Velocity = 54.0 - 49.7 = +4.3 points

Interpretation:
Velocity > +3: Accelerating ✓
Velocity 0 to +3: Steady growth
Velocity < 0: Decelerating
```

---

## 7.3 Momentum Trajectory Forecast

**Simple Linear Projection:**

```
Based on 6-month trend (+1.4 points/month):

Jun: 55
Jul forecast: 56.4
Aug forecast: 57.8
Sep forecast: 59.2

12-month projection: 55 + (1.4 × 12) = 71.8
```

**Caution:** Linear projections assume trend continues. Real momentum can accelerate or decelerate.

---

## 7.4 Volatility Assessment

**Calculate momentum stability:**

```
Monthly scores: 48, 50, 51, 53, 54, 55
Mean: 51.8
Standard Deviation: 2.7

Coefficient of Variation (CV):
CV = (2.7 ÷ 51.8) × 100 = 5.2%

Interpretation:
CV < 10%: Low volatility (stable, predictable) ✓
CV 10-20%: Moderate volatility
CV > 20%: High volatility (erratic, unpredictable)
```

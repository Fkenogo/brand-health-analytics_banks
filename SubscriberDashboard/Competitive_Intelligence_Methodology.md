# 1. COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE FRAMEWORK

## 1.1 What is Competitive Intelligence?

Competitive Intelligence is the systematic collection and analysis of information about:
- **Market dynamics:** Structure, concentration, growth patterns
- **Competitor behavior:** Strategies, strengths, weaknesses
- **Customer preferences:** Why customers choose one bank over another
- **Market opportunities:** Underserved segments and gaps

**Unlike basic competitive analysis** (who's #1 in awareness?), **intelligence** answers:
- Which competitors threaten your growth?
- Where are customers moving?
- What segments can you dominate?
- How should you respond to competitive moves?

---

## 1.2 Intelligence vs Analysis

**Simple Competitive Analysis:**
- "BPR has 75% awareness vs our 72%"
- Static comparison
- Descriptive only

**Competitive Intelligence:**
- "BPR is gaining customers from us at 2:1 ratio, primarily in youth segment, driven by superior digital experience"
- Dynamic understanding
- Actionable insights

---

## 1.3 Key Intelligence Questions

**Market Questions:**
1. How concentrated is the market?
2. Is it growing or consolidating?
3. What's our position and trajectory?

**Competitor Questions:**
4. Who are our real competitors? (Not just obvious ones)
5. What are their strategies?
6. Where are they strong/weak?

**Customer Questions:**
7. Why do customers choose competitors over us?
8. Where are our customers going when they churn?
9. Where are new customers coming from?

**Opportunity Questions:**
10. Which segments are underserved?
11. Where can we win?
12. What moves should we make?

# 2. MARKET STRUCTURE ANALYSIS

## 2.1 Market Share Calculation

### Primary Bank Market Share

**Definition:** Your share of customers who consider you their main bank

**Formula:**
```
Market Share = (Your Preferred Bank count ÷ Total Sample) × 100
```

**Data Source:** Section D, Q3 (Preferred Bank / BUMO)

**Example:**
```
Total sample: 1,000
BK preferred: 108

Market Share = 108 ÷ 1,000 = 10.8%
```

**All Banks:**
```
BPR: 120 (12.0%)  ← Leader
BK: 108 (10.8%)   ← You (#2)
I&M: 80 (8.0%)
EcoBank: 60 (6.0%)
Others: 632 (63.2%)
```

---

## 2.2 Market Concentration

### Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI)

**Purpose:** Measure market concentration (how competitive vs monopolistic)

**Formula:**
```
HHI = Sum of (each firm's market share)²
```

**Example:**
```
BPR: 12.0² = 144
BK: 10.8² = 116.6
I&M: 8.0² = 64
EcoBank: 6.0² = 36
Others: 63.2² = 3,994.2
─────────────────
Total HHI = 4,354.8
```

**Interpretation:**
- **<1,500:** Highly competitive market
- **1,500-2,500:** Moderate concentration
- **>2,500:** Highly concentrated ← Your market

**Your Market Status:** Moderately concentrated, dominated by "Others" (many small players).

---

## 2.3 Market Share Trends

**Track share over time:**

```
Quarter    BK Share   BPR Share   I&M Share
Q1 2025    9.5%       11.0%       7.5%
Q2 2025    10.0%      11.5%       7.8%

```

---

## 2.4 Share of Voice vs Market Share

**Share of Voice:** Your % of top-of-mind mentions

**Rule of Thumb:**
```
SOV > Market Share → Gaining share
SOV = Market Share → Maintaining share
SOV < Market Share → Losing share
```

**Example:**
```
BK Share of Voice: 27.3%
BK Market Share: 10.8%

27.3% > 10.8% → You're building awareness faster than converting
Opportunity: Your high SOV should translate to future share gains
```

# 3. CUSTOMER MOVEMENT PATTERNS

## 3.1 Multi-Banking Behavior

### Average Banks per Customer

**Formula:**
```
For each customer:
    Count active banks (Section D, Q2)
    
Average = Sum of all counts ÷ Number of banking customers
```

**Example:**
```
Customer A: Uses 1 bank
Customer B: Uses 2 banks
Customer C: Uses 3 banks
Customer D: Uses 1 bank
Customer E: Uses 2 banks

Average = (1+2+3+1+2) ÷ 5 = 1.8 banks per customer
```

**Interpretation:**
- 1.0-1.5: Low multi-banking (exclusive relationships)
- 1.6-2.5: Moderate multi-banking ← Typical
- 2.6+: High multi-banking

---

### Multi-Banking Rate

**Formula:**
```
Multi-Banking Rate = (Customers using 2+ banks ÷ All customers) × 100
```

**Example:**
```
Total customers: 400
Single-bank only: 120 (30%)
Multi-bank: 280 (70%)

Multi-Banking Rate = 70%
```

**Strategic Implications:**

**High Multi-Banking (>70%):**
- Market characteristic: Easy to enter
- Your opportunity: Acquire easily
- Your risk: Retain harder, split wallet share

**Low Multi-Banking (<40%):**
- Market characteristic: Sticky relationships
- Your opportunity: Once won, keep longer
- Your challenge: Harder to acquire

---

## 3.2 Competitive Overlap

### Who Shares Your Customers?

**Analysis:** Among YOUR customers, which other banks do they use?

**Data Collection:**
```
For each respondent currently using BK:
    Check: Which OTHER banks selected in "Currently Using" (Section D, Q2)
```

**Example Results:**
```
288 BK customers also use:

I&M: 130 customers (45% overlap)  ← Primary competitor
BPR: 95 customers (33% overlap)
EcoBank: 80 customers (28% overlap)
Access: 40 customers (14% overlap)
BK only: 80 customers (28% exclusive)
```

**Interpretation:**
- **I&M has 45% overlap** → Your primary competitive threat
- **Only 28% exclusive** → Most customers multi-bank
- **I&M customers** are splitting wallets between you

**Strategic Actions:**
- Study I&M: What do they offer that you don't?
- Target I&M users: Convert them to BK-exclusive
- Differentiate: Why choose BK over I&M?

---

# 5. SHARE OF WALLET ANALYSIS

## 5.1 Wallet Share Estimation

**Among multi-bankers, estimate your share:**

**Method 1: Primary Bank Status**
```
Your multi-bank customers: 208
Consider you primary: 28

Primary Rate = 28 ÷ 208 = 13.5%

→ You're primary bank for only 13.5% of multi-bankers
```

**Method 2: Number of Banks Used**
```
If customer uses 2 banks total, you get ~50% wallet
If customer uses 3 banks total, you get ~33% wallet
```

**Weighted Average Wallet Share:**
```
Exclusive users: 80 × 100% = 80 share-points
Dual-bankers: 120 × 50% = 60 share-points
Multi-bankers: 88 × 33% = 29 share-points
────────────────────────────────
Total: 169 share-points from 288 customers
Average: 169 ÷ 288 = 58.7% average wallet share
```

---

## 5.2 Wallet Share by Competitor

**Estimate competitors' wallet share of YOUR customers:**

```
Among 208 BK multi-bank customers:

I&M wallet share: 130 customers × ~50% avg = 65 share-points
BPR wallet share: 95 customers × ~50% avg = 48 share-points
EcoBank wallet share: 80 customers × ~50% avg = 40 share-points

Your remaining wallet: 208 - (estimates) = opportunity
```

**Strategic Goal:** Increase your wallet share from 59% to 75%+.

**Track movement between banks (requires historical data):**

**Period 1 (May):** Record each customer's preferred bank
**Period 2 (June):** Record each customer's preferred bank
**Compare:** Identify switchers

**Example Data:**
```
Respondent 001: May=BPR, June=BK → BK gained from BPR ✓
Respondent 002: May=BK, June=BPR → BK lost to BPR ✗
Respondent 003: May=I&M, June=BK → BK gained from I&M ✓
```

---

## 6.2 Win/Loss Matrix

**Aggregate switching data:**

```
                 Gained From                    Lost To
         BK    BPR   I&M   Eco  |   BK    BPR   I&M   Eco
BK       --    30    20    15   |   --    40    25    10
BPR      40    --    15    20   |   30    --    20    15
I&M      25    20    --    10   |   20    15    --    12
Eco      10    15    12    --   |   15    20    12    --
```

**BK's Net Position:**
```
Gained: 30 (BPR) + 20 (I&M) + 15 (Eco) = 65
Lost: 40 (BPR) + 25 (I&M) + 10 (Eco) = 75

Net: 65 - 75 = -10 customers (losing customers) ⚠️
```

**Where the Problem Is:**
```
BK vs BPR: Gained 30, Lost 40 → Net -10 (biggest threat)
BK vs I&M: Gained 20, Lost 25 → Net -5
BK vs Eco: Gained 15, Lost 10 → Net +5 ✓ (winning)
```

---

## 6.3 Win Rate Calculation

**Formula:**
```
Win Rate vs Competitor X = (Gained from X ÷ (Gained + Lost)) × 100
```

**Example: BK vs BPR**
```
Gained from BPR: 30
Lost to BPR: 40
Total battles: 70

Win Rate = 30 ÷ 70 = 42.9%
Loss Rate = 40 ÷ 70 = 57.1%

→ When customers choose between BK and BPR, BPR wins 57% of the time
```

**Target:** >50% win rate against each competitor.

# 7. COMPETITIVE STRENGTHS & WEAKNESSES

## 7.1 Relative Strength Index

**For each metric, calculate relative performance:**

```
Relative Strength = (Your score - Market average) ÷ Market average × 100
```

**Example: Retention Rate**
```
Your retention: 80%
Market average: 72%

Relative Strength = (80 - 72) ÷ 72 × 100 = +11.1%

→ You're 11% stronger than market in retention ✓
```

**All Metrics:**
```
Metric              You    Market Avg   Relative Strength
Awareness           72%    68%          +5.9% (slight edge)

NPS                 +16    +24          -33% (weak) ⚠️
```

# 10. STRATEGIC RESPONSE FRAMEWORK

## 10.1 Competitive Response Playbook

### When Competitor Launches Price War

**Assess:**
- Can you afford to match?
- Is price your key differentiator?
- What's the long-term impact?

**Response Options:**
1. **Match:** If price-sensitive segment
2. **Differentiate:** Add value instead of cutting price
3. **Segment:** Match in specific segments only
4. **Ignore:** If different target market

**Example:** BPR cuts fees by 20%
- Your Response: Improve digital (your gap), don't compete on price

---

### When Losing Market Share

**Diagnostic Questions:**
1. To whom are you losing share? (Identify competitor)
2. Which segments are leaving? (Age, region, etc.)
3. Why are they leaving? (Switching reasons)
4. Is it temporary or structural?

**Response:**
- Fix root causes (e.g., improve NPS)
- Win-back campaigns for lapsed
- Defend high-value segments
- Differentiate positioning

---

### When Competitor Enters Your Strength

**Example:** Competitor launches better savings rates (your strength)

**Response:**
1. **Defend:** Match or exceed new rates
2. **Expand:** Add features (compound interest, goals)
3. **Communicate:** Remind customers of your advantage
4. **Lock-in:** Create switching costs

---

## 10.2 Strategic Positioning Choices

**Four Generic Strategies:**

**1. Overall Cost Leadership**
- Lowest fees in market
- Efficiency focus
- Mass market appeal

**2. Differentiation**
- Superior service/product
- Premium pricing possible
- Unique value proposition

**3. Focus (Niche)**
- Serve specific segment excellently
- Specialized offerings
- Deep expertise

**4. Hybrid**
- Low cost in some areas
- Differentiated in others
- Example: Low fees + great digital

**Your Current Position:** Hybrid (competitive rates + relationship banking)

## Decision Framework

### When to Attack
- Competitor shows weakness (declining NPS, high churn)
- You have clear advantage in segment
- ROI justifies investment
- Resources available

### When to Defend
- Competitor attacking your strength
- High-value customers at risk
- Core market threatened
- Can't afford to lose

### When to Retreat
- Competitor has overwhelming advantage
- Segment is unprofitable
- Resources better deployed elsewhere
- Strategic refocus needed

### When to Ignore
- Competitor move is in different segment
- No impact on your strategy
- Temporary/tactical move
- Low materiality
